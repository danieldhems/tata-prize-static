The prize

Other prizes include vip trips to the Formula 1 grand prix de monaco 2015

Formula 1 racing is the most technologically advanced and high profile sport on the planet. In a sport where every tenth of a second defines the difference between winning and losing, superior connectivity and content delivery networks are critical components in the quest to win world championships. Innovation. Creativity. Smart use of new technologies. This is what Tata Communications brings to the sport as the Official Connectivity Provider to Formula 1.

In a ground breaking initiative, Tata Communications presents the F1 Connectivity Innovation Prize1 â€“ a trailblazing new contest that will award a grand prize of USD $50,000 to a winning individual or team2. Other prizes include VIP trips to the FORMULA 1 GRAND PRIX DE MONACO 2015.3

To win this highly prestigious competition you will need to apply your creativity and problem-solving skills to at least one of three challenges. The winner of one of the three challenges will then be selected by the judging panel as the grand prizewinner. You can enter each challenge as either an individual or as a part of a team (please refer to entry criteria in the Official Rules).

The F1 Connectivity Innovation Prize is intended to catalyse next-generation technologies by providing a broad canvas from which to inspire imaginative and inventive thinking.

The judges

Click a judge below to reveal more information.

  • John Morrison

    John is a native Scot who graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Heriot Watt University in Edinburgh. He initially worked as a designer of advanced and innovative broadcast electronic hardware but soon moved into broadcast system design being responsible for conceiving the infrastructure required to produce and deliver television and film content for many UK and international production facilities and broadcast networks. Today John has brought his seasoned experience to the role of Chief Technical Officer for Formula One Management, with the responsibility to deliver live television from 19 remote locations a year to over 450 million viewers in 187 territories, thereby making Formula 1 racing the world's most popular annual sporting series.

  • Martin Brundle

    After a noteworthy career in the cockpit, Martin has built a second career in the commentary box and is to many the voice of Formula One, bringing his now-legendary commentary to television audiences around the world courtesy of ITV, the BBC and most recently, SKY Sports. Martin's unrivalled capability in the commentary box is informed by many years in the cockpit, both in Formula 1 with no less than eight teams and 158 starts, but also from a distinguished career in sports car racing that saw him crowned World Sportscar Champion in 1988 and claim a famous victory for Silk Cut Jaguar at Le Mans in 1990. Racing has been his all-consuming passion and in his own words, Formula 1 has seen him 'spill blood, break bones, shed tears, generate tanker loads of sweat, taste the champagne glories and plumb the depths of misery.'

  • Lewis Hamilton

    Lewis Hamilton is rightly regarded as one of the most prodigious talents in global sport, having exploded onto the racetrack in 2007 following an apprenticeship with McLaren that commenced at the tender age of 10. Finishing 2nd in the World Championship in his rookie season and having given witness to his sensational race craft, he claimed the title in 2008. In a finely judged 'off-track' move, Lewis joined the MERCEDES AMG PETRONAS Formula One Team in 2013 and has been a instrumental element of the team's potency since and has recorded 26 wins from 135 GP starts and has appeared on the podium 59 times.
